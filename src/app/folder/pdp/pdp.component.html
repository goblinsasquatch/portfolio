What is the main positive thing you take away from this peer feedback and how will it affect the way you work?

What is the main constructive feedback you take away from this peer feedback and how will it affect the way you work?

I am a firm believer that harsh truths must be told in order to grow, and I welcome it all. That being said, this feedback is bittersweet.


I was completely devastated to read that a team mate feels that I can sometimes be work shy. I can’t think of anything worse. I’ve spoken at length with the person that left this comment and clarified what he meant. During each of our induction projects we have reached a point where the product was ‘done’ and we had some more time before delivery. I have usually advocated for stopping at that point rather than risk breaking the product and failing to impress on the presentation. I recognise that the unrelenting pursuit of excellence requires risk, and that when the situation arises it is always worth a discussion. Additionally, with what we have learned at MS, it’s now a simple case of branching the repo and experimenting without risking the production codebase.


The second part of the comment mentions that I can take the easy route. I can absolutely see this; I do have a tendency to ‘jump to the end’ and implement an obvious 80% solution rather than spend the time to explore other ideas that may be better in the long-run. This is particularly likely when there is a tight deadline – as there often has been during the induction - and I’ve seen something similar before. I recognise that the this unconscious mindset will stifle innovation and will endeavour to take the tactical pause before jumping to a conclusion.


The remainder of the feedback is not surprising to me and aligns closely to feedback received during my 1-1. In an effort to eliminate my overconfidence in my abilities I try to play down my skills rather than self-promote. It’s a difficult balance to strike, and I’d rather err on the side of humility than arrogance.
